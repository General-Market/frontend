If you're deploying capital into crypto, you've probably built a thesis on sectors, narratives, or technology. But what about the founders themselves? Which demographic profiles actually correlate with the highest returns?

We built a database of 4,675 founders across 2,741 crypto projects and cross-referenced it with five years of CoinGecko market cap data. For every token with confirmed founder data, we tracked two things: how many days from listing to all-time high, and the ATH multiplier. Then we sliced the data by every dimension we could — age, gender, nationality, education, team composition, and social media visibility.

The result is a four-part framework for evaluating founding teams — each dimension analyzed through the lens of mimetic capital theory: where the herd goes, alpha dies. Where the herd ignores, alpha lives.

## The Four Signals

### 1. [The Founder Age Signal Nobody Follows](/learn/crypto-founder-age)

The 30-34 bracket is the consensus sweet spot (102 days, 2.8x) — but consensus means inflated entries. The 25-29 bracket returns 3.4x because nobody's patient enough to wait 177 days. The 55+ bracket (17 days, 2.3x) is a mimetic trap — capital has priced it before you can invest.

### 2. [The Nationality Signal the Herd Ignores](/learn/crypto-founder-nationalities)

All-US teams return 2.1x — the lowest of all configurations. Mixed international teams (Has EU, Has CN) return 3.7x. Geography is a proxy for mimetic competition: the more saturated the pipeline, the lower the returns.

### 3. [The PhD Paradox — Education, Gender, and the Real Alpha](/learn/crypto-founder-education-gender)

PhDs reach ATH fastest (66 days) but cap at 1.7x — the market prices them instantly. No-education teams hit 3.5x. Mixed-gender teams return 5.3x vs 2.8x for all-male — the single strongest alpha signal. Intergenerational teams (10-19y age spread) hit 5.9x.

### 4. [The Founder You Can't Google Is the One You Should Back](/learn/crypto-founder-visibility)

58% of Top 100 founders have no LinkedIn. LinkedIn founders return 26x in the Top 100 but invisible founders return 43.6x overall. Visibility is a mimetic premium: a podcast appearance transfers desire, not information. The founder nobody has priced is the definition of alpha.

## The VC Playbook

Based on 4,675 founders and 1,193 tokens with complete TGE-to-ATH data:

1. **Back mixed-gender teams** — 5.3x vs 2.8x is the largest alpha signal
2. **Look for age diversity** — 10-19 year age spread teams return 5.9x
3. **Prefer international teams** — mixed-nationality teams return 3.7x vs 2.1x for all-US
4. **In bull markets**, back 25-34 year-old founders — highest combined speed + multiplier
5. **In bear markets**, back PhD-heavy teams — they outperform when everything is discounted
6. **Invert your due diligence** — the founder you can't google is the one nobody has priced

## Methodology

**Data sources:**
- **Founders:** 2,741 companies and 4,675 founders with manually verified demographic data
- **Market data:** CoinGecko daily market cap snapshots from January 2021 to March 2026, sampled weekly
- **ATH/ATL:** Computed from maximum and minimum historical USD prices per token
- **Performance:** 1,193 tokens with valid TGE price, ATH price, and confirmed founder data

**Matching:** 1,362 of 2,741 companies (49.7%) have CoinGecko IDs. Unmatched companies are typically pre-token, defunct, or unlisted.

**Limitations:**
- Gender data relies on public identification and may not reflect actual identity
- Education data has significant gaps (~40% unknown)
- Survivorship bias affects ATH analysis — we only see tokens that listed on CoinGecko
- Small sample sizes for some categories (All Female n=29, Has CN n=26, 20y+ age spread n=28)
- Correlation is not causation — these are demographic signals, not guaranteed predictors
