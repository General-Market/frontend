We built a database of 4,675 founders across 2,741 crypto projects and cross-referenced it with five years of CoinGecko market cap data. Every week from January 2021 to March 2026, we ranked tokens by market cap, matched them to their founders, and computed demographics for the Top 100, Top 500, and Top 2000.

The tiers use only validated tokens — "Top 100" means the 100 highest-market-cap tokens that we have confirmed founder data for, not the literal CoinGecko top 100. This ensures every data point represents a real, identified founding team.

All ages are adjusted per snapshot year. Someone listed as 40 in 2026 was 35 in 2021. This matters more than you'd think — without this correction, historical age data is systematically wrong.

## Average Founder Age

The average founder age in the Top 100 is 39.7. Top 500 is 39.3. Top 2000 is 38.8.

The gap between tiers is surprisingly small — less than one year separating the founders of the largest projects from the broader market. Crypto doesn't have a dramatic age divide between mega-caps and mid-caps the way traditional tech does.

Over the five-year period, average age has climbed by about 2.8 years across all tiers. Part of this is biological — founders age — but part reflects the maturation of the space. The projects that dominated in 2021 are still largely the same projects dominating in 2026.

## Gender

93% male. Across all tiers. With almost no movement over five years.

This isn't a surprising finding, but the flatness of the trend is striking. Despite the broader tech industry's push toward diversity over this period, crypto founder demographics have barely moved. The Top 100 is 92.9% male. The Top 2000 is 93.1%.

When we look at team composition:

- **All-male teams** make up the vast majority across all tiers
- **Mixed-gender teams** (at least one male and one female co-founder) represent a small single-digit percentage
- **All-female teams** are statistically negligible — fewer than 0.5% in any tier

## Nationality

American founders dominate at roughly 25% of the Top 100. The next largest groups are Chinese, Russian, Canadian, and European nationalities.

- **Top 100:** American dominance is strongest. Projects that achieve mega-cap status skew heavily toward US-based founders.
- **Top 500:** Chinese founders gain share. European representation increases.
- **Top 2000:** More globally distributed. Indian, Korean, and Southeast Asian founders become more visible.

About 15-20% of top projects have mixed-nationality founding teams. Projects with at least one American co-founder tend to cluster in the upper tiers — though causality is unclear.

## Education

PhD holders are overrepresented in the Top 100 relative to the broader market. Protocol-layer founders — the people who designed consensus mechanisms, zero-knowledge systems, and novel cryptographic primitives — disproportionately hold advanced degrees. MIT, Stanford, and ETH Zurich appear most frequently.

## When Do Different Age Groups Peak?

We tracked every token from its first CoinGecko listing (TGE) to its all-time high (ATH) and grouped by the founder's age at the time of TGE.

The 25-29 group takes longest at 177 days but achieves the highest multiplier at 3.4x. The 30-34 group is the fastest at 102 days with a solid 2.8x — the sweet spot of speed and magnitude.

The 55+ group reaches ATH in just 17 days with a 2.3x multiplier — likely survivorship bias, as older founders who launch tokens tend to be well-known figures whose projects attract immediate attention.

## TGE-to-ATH Performance by Team Composition

Beyond individual founder age, we analyzed how team structure correlates with token performance. For each of the 1,193 tokens with valid TGE and ATH data, we classified the founding team by gender mix, nationality composition, age spread, and education level.

### By Gender

Mixed-gender teams hit a median 5.3x ATH multiplier — nearly double the 2.8x of all-male teams. They take longer (145 days vs 97), suggesting sustained growth rather than quick pumps. The sample is small (n=103) but the gap is striking.

### By Nationality

All-Chinese teams reach ATH fastest (70 days, 3.0x). All-US teams are next at 88 days but have the lowest multiplier (2.1x). The highest multipliers come from mixed teams: "Has EU" and "Has CN" both hit 3.7x.

### By Age Spread

Teams with 10-19 years of age spread achieve the highest multiplier at 5.9x — far above any other category. Multi-generational teams (20y+) also show 4.7x. The pattern suggests intergenerational founding teams produce the most explosive returns.

### By Education

All-PhD teams reach ATH fastest (66 days) but have the lowest multiplier (1.7x). They build technically sound projects the market recognizes quickly, but upside is modest. No-education teams hit the highest multiplier at 3.5x — the memecoin and community-project effect.

## ATH and ATL Demographics

**Bull market ATH clusters (Q4 2021, Q4 2024)** show younger average founder ages and higher American representation. These periods produce the most ATH events.

**Bear market ATH clusters (2022-2023)** show fewer tokens hitting ATH, but those that do tend to have older founders with stronger educational backgrounds. Bear market outperformance correlates with technical depth.

**ATL patterns** show roughly inverse demographics. Bear market ATL clusters are dominated by younger founders with less documented education.

## Methodology

**Data sources:**
- **Founders:** 2,741 companies and 4,675 founders with manually verified demographic data
- **Market data:** CoinGecko daily market cap snapshots from January 2021 to March 2026, sampled weekly
- **ATH/ATL:** Computed from maximum and minimum historical USD prices per token
- **Performance:** 1,193 tokens with valid TGE price, ATH price, and confirmed founder data

**Matching:** 1,362 of 2,741 companies (49.7%) have CoinGecko IDs. Unmatched companies are typically pre-token, defunct, or unlisted.

**Limitations:**
- Gender data relies on public identification and may not reflect actual identity
- Education data has significant gaps (~40% unknown)
- Survivorship bias affects ATH analysis
- Small sample sizes for some categories (All Female n=29, Has CN n=26, 20y+ age spread n=28)
