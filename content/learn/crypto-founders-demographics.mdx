If you're deploying capital into crypto, you've probably built a thesis on sectors, narratives, or technology. But what about the founders themselves? Which demographic profiles actually correlate with the highest returns?

We built a database of 4,675 founders across 2,741 crypto projects and cross-referenced it with five years of CoinGecko market cap data. For every token with confirmed founder data, we tracked two things: how many days from listing to all-time high, and the ATH multiplier. Then we sliced the data by every dimension we could — age, gender, nationality, education, team composition.

The result is a practical framework for evaluating founding teams. Not all signals are actionable (you can't control a founder's age), but the patterns are clear enough to inform allocation decisions.

## The Return Profile by Founder Age

The data answers a question VCs have debated forever: is there an ideal founder age?

In crypto, the **30-34 bracket** is the sweet spot. They reach ATH fastest (102 days median) with a respectable 2.8x multiplier. They've had enough time to build technical depth and professional networks, but are still hungry enough to execute aggressively.

The **25-29 group** achieves the highest multiplier (3.4x) but takes longest (177 days) — they build ambitious projects that take time to mature. If you're patient capital, this is the best risk-adjusted bet.

The **55+ group** hits ATH in just 17 days at 2.3x — but this is survivorship bias. These are established figures (think Silvio Micali, Charles Hoskinson) whose projects attract immediate capital. By the time you can invest, the easy gains are gone.

## The Team Composition Edge

Individual founder demographics matter, but **team composition is where the strongest signals live.**

**Mixed-gender teams return 5.3x vs 2.8x for all-male.** This is the single largest demographic alpha in our dataset. Mixed teams take longer (145 days vs 97) — they build for sustained growth, not quick pumps. The sample is modest (n=103) but the gap is too large to ignore. For VCs, this suggests that mixed-gender founding teams are systematically underpriced.

**Intergenerational teams (10-19 year age spread) hit 5.9x** — the highest of any category. Teams of 20y+ spread show 4.7x. The pattern is clear: founding teams that combine a senior builder with a younger technical co-founder produce explosive returns. Same-age teams (0-4y spread) are average at 2.9x.

**Mixed-nationality teams outperform homogeneous ones.** "Has EU" and "Has CN" both hit 3.7x. All-US teams have the lowest multiplier (2.1x) despite reaching ATH second-fastest (88 days). American founders build recognized brands quickly but the upside is priced in. International founding teams have better risk-reward.

## The Education Paradox

**All-PhD teams reach ATH fastest (66 days) but have the lowest multiplier (1.7x).** High floor, low ceiling. They build technically sound protocols that the market prices correctly and quickly — there's no information asymmetry to exploit.

**No-education teams hit the highest multiplier at 3.5x.** This is the memecoin and community-project effect — wild variance with massive upside for survivors. For VCs, these are lottery tickets: most fail, but the ones that work return 10x+.

**The practical play:** Mixed education teams (some PhDs, some without degrees) combine technical credibility with community-building instinct. They're the middle ground between the PhD's low ceiling and the dropout's high variance.

## Who Dominates the Top Tiers?

American founders hold ~25% of the Top 500 and that share is rising — from 23% in 2022 to 28% in 2026. Chinese founders peaked at 14% in early 2021 and have declined to ~12%. Stanford is surging past MIT as the most represented university.

The regional composition has been remarkably stable. Americas at ~50%, Europe ~25%, Asia ~20%. Geography is sticky in crypto — the projects that made it into the top tiers early have stayed there.

## Bull vs Bear Market Signals

**Bull market ATH clusters (Q4 2021, Q4 2024)** produce the most ATH events and skew toward younger founders with higher American representation. These are the windows where new projects break through.

**Bear market ATH clusters (2022-2023)** are rare but reveal a different profile: older founders, stronger educational backgrounds, more PhDs. Bear market outperformance correlates with technical depth. For VCs, this means the best bear market bets are protocol-layer teams with deep academic credentials — they're the ones building through the downturn.

## The VC Playbook

Based on 4,675 founders and 1,193 tokens with complete TGE-to-ATH data:

1. **Back mixed-gender teams** — 5.3x vs 2.8x is the largest alpha signal
2. **Look for age diversity** — 10-19 year age spread teams return 5.9x
3. **Prefer international teams** — mixed-nationality teams (especially "Has EU" or "Has CN") return 3.7x vs 2.1x for all-US
4. **In bull markets**, back 25-34 year-old founders — highest combined speed + multiplier
5. **In bear markets**, back PhD-heavy teams — they outperform when the market is discounting everything
6. **Avoid all-male, all-American, same-age teams** — they're the median outcome (2.1-2.8x), not the outlier

## Methodology

**Data sources:**
- **Founders:** 2,741 companies and 4,675 founders with manually verified demographic data
- **Market data:** CoinGecko daily market cap snapshots from January 2021 to March 2026, sampled weekly
- **ATH/ATL:** Computed from maximum and minimum historical USD prices per token
- **Performance:** 1,193 tokens with valid TGE price, ATH price, and confirmed founder data

**Matching:** 1,362 of 2,741 companies (49.7%) have CoinGecko IDs. Unmatched companies are typically pre-token, defunct, or unlisted.

**Limitations:**
- Gender data relies on public identification and may not reflect actual identity
- Education data has significant gaps (~40% unknown)
- Survivorship bias affects ATH analysis — we only see tokens that listed on CoinGecko
- Small sample sizes for some categories (All Female n=29, Has CN n=26, 20y+ age spread n=28)
- Correlation is not causation — these are demographic signals, not guaranteed predictors
